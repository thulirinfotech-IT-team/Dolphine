Abstract

Dolphin Naturals is a full-stack e-commerce web application developed to facilitate the online buying and selling of herbal and natural wellness products. The platform is built using React.js on the frontend and FastAPI (Python) on the backend, with MongoDB as the primary database, following a three-tier client-server architecture.

The system provides a complete customer-facing storefront that includes product browsing with category and search filtering, detailed product pages with multiple images, ingredients, benefits, usage instructions, a shopping cart with quantity management, a checkout flow with shipping and tax calculation, and a product review system with star ratings and verified purchase badges. Products support multiple quantity variants (e.g., 50g, 100g, 250g) with independent pricing and stock levels.

User authentication is implemented using a two-factor approach — credential verification followed by a One-Time Password (OTP) sent via email — with JWT (JSON Web Token) used for secure session management. Password security is handled using Bcrypt hashing.

A comprehensive Admin Panel allows administrators to manage the entire platform, including full CRUD operations on products, categories, promotional banners, and doctor educational videos. The application also supports direct file uploads for product images and videos, stored on the local filesystem and served as static assets.

Problem Identification

The herbal and natural wellness product industry in India has seen significant growth in recent years, yet most small and medium-scale herbal product businesses continue to rely heavily on physical stores, word-of-mouth, and social media messaging for sales. This creates several critical problems:

  • No Online Presence: Herbal product sellers lack a dedicated, professional e-commerce platform, making it difficult for customers to discover and purchase products online.
  • Manual Order Management: Orders are managed manually via phone calls or chat apps, leading to errors, delays, and poor customer experience.
  • Limited Product Information: Customers cannot access detailed product information such as ingredients, benefits, usage instructions, or customer reviews before purchasing.
  • No Secure Authentication: Existing informal sales channels lack secure login mechanisms, making customer data vulnerable.
  • Lack of Admin Control: Business owners have no centralised dashboard to manage products, inventory, promotions, and customer content in real time.
  • No Expert Guidance: Customers have no access to doctor or expert recommendations related to herbal products, reducing trust and purchase confidence.

These problems collectively result in lost sales opportunities, poor customer satisfaction, and an inability to scale the business effectively in the digital marketplace.

Objective of the Project

The primary objective of Dolphin Naturals is to develop a fully functional, secure, and user-friendly e-commerce web application that bridges the gap between herbal product sellers and customers. The specific objectives are:

  • 1. Online Product Marketplace: Build a complete online store where customers can browse, search, filter, and purchase herbal and natural wellness products with ease.
  • 2. Secure Authentication: Implement a two-factor authentication system using OTP (One-Time Password) and JWT tokens to ensure secure access for all users.
  • 3. Seamless Shopping Experience: Provide a smooth end-to-end shopping journey — from product discovery to cart management, checkout, and order placement — with support for multiple payment modes.
  • 4. Detailed Product Information: Display comprehensive product details including images, benefits, ingredients, usage instructions, quantity variants, and customer reviews to assist informed purchasing decisions.
  • 5. Comprehensive Admin Panel: Develop a powerful admin dashboard for managing products, categories, banners, doctor videos, and platform content without requiring technical knowledge.
  • 6. Expert Content Integration: Integrate a doctor video section to provide customers with expert guidance on herbal products, increasing trust and credibility of the platform.
  • 7. Scalable and Maintainable Architecture: Design the system using modern technologies (React.js, FastAPI, MongoDB) to ensure the platform is scalable, maintainable, and ready for future enhancements.
Existing System and its Drawbacks

Existing System

Currently, most small herbal product businesses in India operate through one or more of the following informal channels:

  • Physical Retail Stores: Products are sold exclusively through brick-and-mortar shops, limiting reach to local customers only.
  • WhatsApp & Social Media: Orders are taken via WhatsApp messages or Instagram/Facebook DMs, with no formal order tracking or confirmation system.
  • Third-Party Marketplaces: Some sellers list products on platforms like Amazon or Flipkart but pay high commissions and have no control over their brand experience.
  • Basic Static Websites: Some businesses have static HTML websites that display products but have no shopping cart, payment, or order management functionality.

Modules Identification and Description

The Dolphin Naturals application is divided into the following major modules, each handling a distinct functional area of the system:

Module 1 — User Authentication & Authorization

Handles all user identity management including registration, login, OTP verification, password reset, and JWT-based session management. Role-based access control restricts admin features to authorised users only. Bcrypt is used for secure password storage.

Module 2 — Product Catalog

Manages all product data including name, description, pricing (MRP, sale price, discount), stock, multiple images, tags, benefits, ingredients, how-to-use, and quantity variants. Supports search and category-based filtering for easy product discovery.

Module 3 — Shopping Cart

Allows authenticated users to add products (with optional variant selection) to their cart, update quantities, remove items, and view running totals. Cart data is persisted in MongoDB and associated with the user's account for continuity across sessions.

Module 4 — Order & Checkout

Processes customer orders by collecting delivery information (name, address, mobile, pincode), calculating subtotal, shipping charges (free above ₹2000), and 5% tax. Supports UPI and Cash on Delivery (COD) payment methods. Orders are stored in MongoDB for tracking.

Module 5 — Reviews & Ratings

Enables authenticated users to submit product reviews with 1–5 star ratings, review titles, and comments. Displays verified purchase badges, review breakdowns, average ratings, and helpful vote counts. Each user is limited to one review per product.

Module 6 — Admin Panel

A protected admin dashboard providing full CRUD operations for products, categories, banners, and doctor videos. Admins can control homepage visibility (max 4 products/categories featured), manage file uploads, toggle active status, and set display order for categories and videos.

Module 7 — Banner & Promotions

Manages promotional homepage banners with title, subtitle, call-to-action button (text + link), and banner image. Admins can create, update, and toggle banners active or inactive. Active banners appear in the homepage carousel with auto-slide functionality.

Module 8 — Doctor Videos

Provides an educational video section where admins can upload doctor-recommended content (YouTube links or locally uploaded videos). Each video includes doctor name, designation, description, thumbnail, and duration. Videos are displayed in a grid on the home page to build customer trust.

Module 9 — File Upload

Handles secure file uploads for product images (max 5MB: JPG, PNG, GIF, WebP) and doctor videos (max 100MB: MP4, MOV, AVI, MKV, WebM). Files are validated for type and size, stored with unique UUID-based filenames, and served as static assets via FastAPI.

Methodology to be Adopted

The development of the Dolphin Naturals platform follows the Agile Development Methodology, specifically an iterative and incremental approach. This methodology was chosen due to its flexibility, continuous feedback integration, and suitability for web application development where requirements may evolve over time.
